My Journey

I started by taking apart computers as a teenager — not always knowing how to put them back together, but always curious about how things worked. That same mindset led me to the high seas, where I worked as a marine engineer keeping massive ship engines running across oceans.

But beneath the engine grease, my interest in systems — especially decentralized ones — kept growing. I discovered Bitcoin in its early days and was fascinated not just by the asset, but by the architecture. That rabbit hole led to trading, investing, and eventually a complete pivot: I began studying computer science, data systems, and blockchain technology.

Over the last few years, I've transitioned fully into tech, working as a crypto data engineer building pipelines that power digital asset analytics. From building ETL flows for real-time exchange data to designing dashboards tracking liquidity, I now work at the intersection of markets and infrastructure.

I still love complexity — whether it's mechanical or mathematical — but today, the engine rooms I work on live in code, APIs, and blockchains.
